Color Wheel and Color Theory

Color Combinations

The color wheel is a visual representation of colors arranged according to their chromatic relationship. It helps to understand and create color combinations based on complementary, analogous, and triadic color schemes.

  • Complementary Colors: Colors located opposite each other on the color wheel. They create high contrast and vibrant looks. For example, red and green.
  • Analogous Colors: Colors located next to each other on the color wheel. They create serene and comfortable designs. For example, blue, blue-green, and green.
  • Triadic Colors: Colors that are evenly spaced around the color wheel. They provide a balanced and harmonious color scheme. For example, red, yellow, and blue.

Color Theory

Color theory explores how colors interact with one another and how they can be used in design to convey different emotions and messages. Key concepts include:

  • Primary Colors: The three primary colors are red, blue, and yellow. They cannot be created by mixing other colors.
  • Secondary Colors: These are created by mixing two primary colors. For example, green (blue + yellow), orange (red + yellow), and purple (red + blue).
  • Tertiary Colors: These are formed by mixing a primary color with a secondary color. For example, red-orange, yellow-green, and blue-purple.
